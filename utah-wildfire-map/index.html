Wildfires in Utah right now

Utah's wildfires burn across pinyon-juniper, sagebrush, and mountain forests, often ignited by lightning or human activity. Hot, dry summers and steep terrain can drive rapid fire growth in the state's rural counties. Wildfire season here typically runs June through September.

Where Utah wildfires burn

The west desert and central counties — Juab, Millard, Beaver, Iron — carry Utah's big rangeland fires, while the Wasatch and southern mountain forests see steep, terrain-driven timber fires. The Wasatch Front's canyon interface makes even small ignitions consequential near Salt Lake City.

Frequently asked questions

How many wildfires are burning in Utah right now?
As of the latest update, about 13 active wildfires are being tracked in Utah, covering roughly 252,645 acres across 10 counties.
What is the largest wildfire in Utah?
The Babylon Fire in San Juan County is currently the largest tracked fire in Utah at about 106,610 acres (50% contained).
When is wildfire season in Utah?
Utah’s wildfire season typically runs June through September. Risk is highest during hot, dry, and windy stretches, so check current conditions and any local burn restrictions before outdoor activity.
Where can I get official Utah wildfire and evacuation information?
For official updates and evacuation orders, contact Utah Division of Forestry, Fire & State Lands, check InciWeb, and follow your county emergency management and local authorities.
