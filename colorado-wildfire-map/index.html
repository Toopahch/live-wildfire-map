Wildfires in Colorado right now

Colorado has experienced several of its largest and most damaging wildfires in recent years, including fast-moving grass fires along the Front Range urban interface. High elevation, dry air, and wind make containment challenging. Wildfire season here typically runs May through September (with spring wind-driven grass fires).

Where Colorado wildfires burn

The Front Range urban interface from Fort Collins to Colorado Springs faces the highest life-and-property risk, while the largest burns have come in the northern mountains and the Western Slope. Downslope wind events can push winter and spring grass fires into neighborhoods with little warning.

Frequently asked questions

How many wildfires are burning in Colorado right now?
As of the latest update, about 14 active wildfires are being tracked in Colorado, covering roughly 208,774 acres across 11 counties.
What is the largest wildfire in Colorado?
The Aspen Acres Fire in Custer County is currently the largest tracked fire in Colorado at about 98,752 acres (36% contained).
When is wildfire season in Colorado?
Colorado’s wildfire season typically runs May through September (with spring wind-driven grass fires). Risk is highest during hot, dry, and windy stretches, so check current conditions and any local burn restrictions before outdoor activity.
Where can I get official Colorado wildfire and evacuation information?
For official updates and evacuation orders, contact Colorado Division of Fire Prevention & Control, check InciWeb, and follow your county emergency management and local authorities.
