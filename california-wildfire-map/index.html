Wildfires in California right now

California sees some of the largest and most destructive wildfires in the United States, driven by prolonged drought, dense fuels, and wind events such as the Santa Ana and Diablo winds. CAL FIRE and federal partners coordinate response across the state's diverse terrain, from coastal ranges to the Sierra Nevada. Wildfire season here typically runs May through October (peaking late summer and fall with offshore winds).

Where California wildfires burn

Activity concentrates in the Sierra Nevada foothills and the Shasta–Trinity region in the north, the chaparral of Southern California from Ventura to San Diego, and the wildland-urban interface around the Bay Area and wine country. Offshore wind corridors — Santa Ana in the south, Diablo in the north — produce the fastest-moving and most destructive fires.

Frequently asked questions

How many wildfires are burning in California right now?
As of the latest update, about 31 active wildfires are being tracked in California, covering roughly 93,248 acres across 16 counties.
What is the largest wildfire in California?
The Biscar Fire in Lassen County is currently the largest tracked fire in California at about 69,207 acres (5% contained).
When is wildfire season in California?
California’s wildfire season typically runs May through October (peaking late summer and fall with offshore winds). Risk is highest during hot, dry, and windy stretches, so check current conditions and any local burn restrictions before outdoor activity.
Where can I get official California wildfire and evacuation information?
For official updates and evacuation orders, contact CAL FIRE, check InciWeb, and follow your county emergency management and local authorities.
