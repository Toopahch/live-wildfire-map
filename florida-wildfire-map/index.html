Wildfires in Florida right now

Florida's wildfires burn in pine flatwoods, palmetto, and swamp during the dry season. Prescribed fire is widely used to reduce fuels, and lightning-ignited fires are common in the state's fire-adapted ecosystems. Wildfire season here typically runs winter through spring (the dry season).

Where Florida wildfires burn

The pine flatwoods and palmetto of central and south Florida — and drained swamp margins near the Everglades — burn most often, with dry-season fires occasionally threatening the I-4 and coastal growth corridors. Florida also runs one of the nation's largest prescribed-fire programs, so not all smoke means wildfire.

Frequently asked questions

How many wildfires are burning in Florida right now?
As of the latest update, about 14 active wildfires are being tracked in Florida, covering roughly 40,676 acres across 11 counties.
What is the largest wildfire in Florida?
The Quarry 2 Fire in Miami-Dade County is currently the largest tracked fire in Florida at about 19,018 acres (97% contained).
When is wildfire season in Florida?
Florida’s wildfire season typically runs winter through spring (the dry season). Risk is highest during hot, dry, and windy stretches, so check current conditions and any local burn restrictions before outdoor activity.
Where can I get official Florida wildfire and evacuation information?
For official updates and evacuation orders, contact Florida Forest Service, check InciWeb, and follow your county emergency management and local authorities.
